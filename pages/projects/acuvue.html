ACUVUE® Go!

Mobile UX • Prototyping • Interaction Design

Client: ACUVUE®

Class: Information Technology & Web Science Capstone Group Project

Role: UI/UX Designer/Developer

Duration: September 2024 - December 2024

Background

ACUVUE® is a premium contact lens brand manufactured by Johnson & Johnson. ACUVUE® services millions of people worldwide providing them with a premium contact lens experience. Additionally, ACUVUE® collaborates with eye care professionals to update them on the latest advancements in eye health. ACUVUE® is a leader in the contact lens industry bringing innovation and technological advancements to millions of patients worldwide

Challenge

ACUVUE® is looking for a way to effectively differentiate itself from competitors, show the value and superiority of their product, and ensure optometrists entering the professional space use ACUVUE® lenses. ACUVUE® contact lenses focus on comfort and design but come at a premium price. Additionally, once professionals start recommending and utilizing a brand, they are unlikely to switch.

Objectives

  • Build a mobile application with mini games and interactive elements optomery students can use at a conference.
  • Design the experience to remain in the user’s browser so they don’t need to download an external application.

My Role

I developed the high fidelity wireframes for the client using Figma and ensured that the design of the application matched current ACUVUE® design standards. Additionally, I led development on the front-end of the application generating the ideas for the games and implementing them. I also worked on connecting the front-end solution to the back-end ensuring all information could correctly be saved to the the database.

Development

The front-end of the application was built using React.js and CSS. The back-end was developed using Node.js and MongoDB®. The final application included a sign up page and a main page for users to view activities. The application included five mini games: trivia, anagrams, phrase guesser, odd one out, and memory. A fedback survey was also included. Two interactive elements including a QR code for visiting the ACUVUE® booth and attending a talk were included as interactive elements. A rewards shop was also included to encourage users to continue playing to earn prizes.

Future Steps

The application is approved to be launched at an upcoming optomery conference.

Lessons Learned

  • Beware of scope creep, particularly for projects with a quick turnaround time.
  • Always have a particular audience you’re designing for.
